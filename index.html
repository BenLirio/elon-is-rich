Chapter 01 · Calibration

First, your brain needs recalibrating.

"Million," "billion," and "trillion" rhyme, so your brain files them next to each other. They are not next to each other. Here's a way to feel the gap: imagine counting one dollar every second — no sleep, no breaks, all day and all night. How long to count each fortune?

$1 million, at $1/sec

11.6 days

Annoying, but you'd live.

$1 billion, at $1/sec

31.7 years

Start at college graduation, finish in your mid-fifties.

$1 trillion, at $1/sec

31,688 years

You'd have needed to start during the Ice Age — fourteen millennia before the cave paintings at Lascaux.

1,000,000 s = 11.57 days · 1,000,000,000 s = 31.7 years · 1,000,000,000,000 s = 31,688 years. The gap between a millionaire and Elon is the gap between 12 days and 300 centuries.

Chapter 07 · Geopolitics

Heavier than nations.

One man's net worth versus the entire annual economic output of countries (GDP, IMF 2025–26).

≈ Fort Knox ×1.0

His fortune roughly equals the entire US official gold reserve — all 8,133 tonnes, Fort Knox included — at June 2026 prices (~$1.10T).

3.4% of US GDP

Rockefeller peaked at ~2.3% in 1913. By share of the economy, Elon is now the richest American who has ever lived.

45¢ of every $1

His net worth equals 45% of all physical US currency in circulation — every bill in every wallet, register, and vault on Earth ($2.32T).

GDP is yearly output, not wealth — the comparison flatters the countries, and he still wins. Gold: 261.5M oz × $4,216/oz. Currency: Fed, Dec 2024. Rockefeller: Guinness World Records / US GDP 1913.

Chapter 09 · Closing entries

Four last attempts to fathom it.

66,300,000 years

Working full-time at federal minimum wage ($7.25/hr) to earn $1T. Clock in alongside the last T. rex.

2,740 years

How long $1T lasts at $1,000,000 a day. Start before Rome is founded; outlast it by millennia.

$126 per human

He could hand every one of the 8.3 billion people alive $126 in cash. For ~2.6 billion people, that's more than a week's income.

25 years

How long he could fund the UN's $40B/yr plan to end world hunger — using under 4% of his fortune per year. This one isn't a joke.
